# Industry Patterns (Reference)

## Manufacturing
- Higher working capital cycles; inventory and receivable days typically 60-120 days.
- EBITDA margins 8-15% typical; leverage tolerance moderate (Debt/EBITDA 2-3x).
- Seasonality common; assess peak vs off-peak bank flows.

## Trading / Distribution
- Lower margins (3-8%); high turnover velocity.
- GST-bank reconciliation critical due to cash sales mix.
- Thin equity base common; monitor related-party funding.

## Services / IT
- Lower working capital intensity; receivable days 30-60.
- Higher EBITDA margins possible (15-30%).
- Lower fixed asset base; human capital risk dominant.

## Construction / Infrastructure
- High working capital; retention money and long receivable cycles.
- Project-based revenue; assess order book and completion certificates.
- Leverage often higher; covenant monitoring essential.

## Retail / Consumer
- Seasonal spikes (festive); inventory turnover key metric.
- Moderate margins; franchise/royalty outflows to watch.

## Generic Overlay
When industry is unknown, apply conservative thresholds: current ratio >=1.0, interest coverage >=1.5x, no unexplained cross-source gaps >30%.
