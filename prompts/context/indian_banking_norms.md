# Indian Banking Norms (Reference)

## Current Account Conduct
- **ADB floor:** Portfolio typically expects minimum average daily balance; sustained breach indicates liquidity stress.
- **Top-3 concentration:** Credit concentration above 60% from top 3 counterparties is a watch-out; above 70% is a red flag for MSME portfolios.
- **Cash share:** Cash deposits exceeding 25% of total credits may indicate informal/unreported revenue or tax evasion risk.
- **Return rates:** Cheque/NACH return rates above 5% trigger RBI out-of-order proxy concerns.
- **Credit gap:** Gaps between credit inflows exceeding 30 days without explanation warrant probe.

## Overdraft Conduct
- **Utilization:** Sustained utilization above 80% indicates dependency on OD facility.
- **Days over limit:** Any days over sanctioned limit are material red flags.
- **Interest coverage:** 90-day interest debits vs operating cash inflows; coverage below 1.0x is critical.

## Statutory & Compliance
- GST filing delays beyond 1 month are compliance red flags.
- Statutory payment misses (PF, TDS, GST) visible in bank debits indicate governance risk.

## Cross-Source Norms
- GST outward turnover vs bank credit turnover gap >30% requires explanation.
- Bureau DPD vs bank return behavior inconsistency suggests hidden stress.
